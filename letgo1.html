🔡 The English Alphabet A–Z
💡 Articles: "a" vs "an"
Rule: Use "a" or "an"?
Use a before consonant sounds: a pencil · a book · a bag
Use an before vowel sounds (A,E,I,O,U): an eraser · an apple
💡 Vowels = A E I O U → use "an" before these sounds!
🔢 Numbers 1–10
🏃 What can you do?
Grammar: "I can ___"
I can + verb = I am able to do something
I can walk. / I can run. / I can jump. / I can dance.
Question: "What can you do?"
What can you do? → I can ___.
Structure: What + can + subject + do?

3. Yes/No Questions: "Is this a ___?"
Is this a book? → Yes, it is. / No, it isn't.

4. All Contractions in Unit 1
What is → What's It is → It's I am → I'm is not → isn't

🔷 Shapes
Grammar: Adjective + Noun (Color + Shape)
Separate: This is a star. It's blue.
Combined: It's a blue star.
In English, adjectives come BEFORE the noun!
Is this a red square? → Yes, it is. / No, it isn't.
✏️ Grammar Focus — Unit 2
1. Adjective Order: Color + Shape
Color + Shape: a blue star · a red square · a yellow circle
⚠️ WRONG: "a star blue" | ✅ RIGHT: "a blue star"
2. Combining Two Sentences
This is a star. It's blue. → It's a blue star.
3. Commands
Pick up the pencil. / Draw a circle.
Imperatives: verb only, no subject needed
4. Song vocabulary: The Black Cat Song
This is a black cat. This is a yellow and pink pencil.

✏️ Writing Supplies (Singular & Plural)
Grammar: Singular → Plural (add -s)
a crayon → crayons · a notebook → notebooks
How many crayons? → One crayon. / Three crayons.

💻 Electronics
Grammar: "What are these?" / "They're ___."
Singular: What's this? → It's a cell phone.
Plural: What are these? → They're CDs.
They are → They're
✏️ Grammar Focus — Unit 3
1. Singular → Plural
crayon→crayons · marker→markers · notebook→notebooks
2. "How many ___?" Counting
How many crayons? → One crayon. / Three crayons.
Use "How many" for countable plural nouns.
3. This / These → It / They
This is a CD. → It's a CD. (singular)
These are CDs. → They're CDs. (plural)
4. Commands: point to, find, count
Point to the duck. / Find the cat. / Count the stars.

👀 Describing People
Grammar: He's/She's + adjective

✏️ Grammar Focus — Unit 4
1. Subject Pronouns (He / She)
He = male: grandfather, father, brother
She = female: grandmother, mother, sister
2. Possessive "my"
my + family: my grandmother · my father · my sister
"my" = belonging to me
3. Who questions
Who's she? → She's my grandmother. She's old.
Who's he? → He's my grandfather. He's tall.
4. Describing adjectives (opposites)
tall ↔ short · young ↔ old · pretty · handsome
Commands: say, count, read, write

📍 Location Words (Prepositions)
📦ininside something
📤onon top of something
📥underbelow something
↔️bynext to something
Grammar: "Where's the ___?"
Where's the bat? → It's on the table.
Where are the balls? → They're under the table.

Song: "Where Are the Bugs?"
Commands: put on, put by, put in, put under
✏️ Grammar Focus — Unit 6
1. There is / There are
Singular: There's a flower. / There is a cloud.
Plural: There are four trees.
1 thing = "there is" · 2+ things = "there are"
2. Prepositions of Place
in · on · under · by (next to)
The bat is ON the table. The ball is UNDER the bench.
3. Where questions (singular/plural)
Singular: Where's the bat? → It's on the table.
Plural: Where are the balls? → They're under the table.

🦁 Zoo Animals
Grammar: "I like / I don't like ___."
I like monkeys. / I don't like lions.
Zoo actions: hear · see · feed · pet
✏️ Grammar Focus — Unit 8
1. I like / I don't like + plural
I like cats. / I don't like lions.
Always use plural after "I like": I like dogs (not "a dog")
2. Favorite Questions
What's your favorite color/animal?
Short: Red. / Blue. OR Full: My favorite color is red.
3. "What about you?" — Returning the question
A: Red. What about you? B: I like blue.
A polite way to ask the same question back!
4. Animal Action Verbs
hear · see · feed · pet
I can hear the lion. / I can pet the rabbit.
